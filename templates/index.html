Q & A:

Q1: What is this site?

HLS Course Ratings is basically what the title suggests. It presents data gathered (by a script) about instructors' ratings.

The ratings are based on student evaluations.

Q2: What kinds of data are displayed?

For now, this website contains:

  1. the professor's name,
  2. the course being evaluated,
  3. the year/semester of the course,
  4. the number of student evaluations for that course, and
  5. the average rating of that professor's "effectiveness" in the course.

For now, only evaluations

  1. of courses taught by full-time HLS professors, and
  2. were taught on or after 2013
Are included. Some courses might be omitted in the process of data-extraction.

Q3: Where did the data come from?

The data was gathered from HLS student evaluations. HLS Students may log in from the link to access the complete evaluations.

Q4: When was the data gathered?

The data was last updated on Dec. 3, 2018.
